So, you might be asking, why is documentation so important that someone would write a talk about it. Well, basically, it all sums down to this: no one wants to be handed off a "blurrg". [click] Here, in the first episode of the Mandalorian, Mando gets one vehicle to go through the Badlands: A beast named a blurrg. This wild mount throws him almost instantly off his back. Touching scene that show Mando as vulnerable, great show if you have not seen it yet... Back to components: nobody wants to have to tame the components before use. What interrests me more in this scene is what Mando says just afterwards: [click] Uh! I don't have time for this! Don't you have a land speeder or a speeder bike I can hire? We all are in the same situation when consuming components. We want the speeder bike, not the blurrg... Well, I know how cute they are... In this case, it often takes more time to write the documentation than to tame the components. [click] Sarah still wants to do her job right. She still needs to document the components. What do we do when we have a repetitive task? What are our computers best feature? What can they do well that we usually can't?

Oh! I don't have time for this either!

Introducing your first documentation tool: Storybook. Who here has never used storybook? Never heard of it? [click] You can think of storybook as your TDD for visual components. It allows us to build units and test in isolation from the rest of the app. This way we can make sure that at least the component looks like what we want to. Let me show you how it works. [Demo]
